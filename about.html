About Green Day

Origins and Breakthrough

Green Day was formed in 1987 in East Bay, California, by Billie Joe Armstrong and Mike Dirnt. Originally named Sweet Children, they adopted the name Green Day and quickly gained attention in the underground punk scene.

Dookie and Mainstream Success

In 1994, the release of their third album Dookie launched them into international fame, selling over 10 million copies. The album featured hits like “Basket Case” and “When I Come Around.”

Reinvention with American Idiot

After a dip in popularity, Green Day returned in 2004 with American Idiot, a rock opera critiquing American politics and culture. It became a cultural phenomenon and earned them a Grammy.

Legacy and Influence

Green Day is one of the most influential bands in punk rock history. They've sold over 75 million records worldwide and were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2015.

Band Members

Billie Joe Armstrong – Lead vocals, guitar
Mike Dirnt – Bass guitar, backing vocals
Tré Cool – Drums, percussion
